A multivariate statistical study of red and white wine chemistry. MANOVA tests
which chemical attributes most strongly differentiate the two types, while supervised
models — Random Forest, SVM, and Logistic Regression — push classification accuracy
to roughly 99% with the Random Forest.

K-means clustering rounds out the analysis to surface quality tiers that aren't
obvious from the raw labels. The end result is a compact account of what actually
drives the difference between a red and a white in measurable terms.
