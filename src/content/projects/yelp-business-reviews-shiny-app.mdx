## What it does

Three layers, all interactive:

1. **Map exploration.** Filter businesses on a `leaflet` map by category, rating,
   credit-card acceptance, and operating hours. The right-hand panel updates live with
   summary stats for the visible subset.
2. **Spatial autocorrelation.** Tune the variogram parameters — sill, range, nugget,
   and the kappa for Matern models — and watch the empirical and fitted variograms
   redraw in real time.
3. **Kriged surface.** Once a variogram is fitted, the app produces a kriged surface
   estimating ratings across the region, with a credibility band so you can see where
   the prediction is well-supported versus interpolated out of nothing.

## Why it works for teaching

Spatial autocorrelation is much easier to feel than to read about. The dashboard puts
the knobs directly in the user's hands — change the range, watch the surface get
smoother; lower the nugget, watch the variance band tighten. For classroom use it
replaces a 40-minute lecture with about 5 minutes of poking around.

## Implementation notes

- **Stack:** R Shiny + `leaflet` + `gstat` + `sf` for the spatial pipeline.
- **Variogram fitting** uses weighted least squares on the empirical variogram, with
  the fit refit whenever the user changes parameters. Sliders are debounced so the
  fitting doesn't lag the UI.
- **Cached kriging.** Surfaces are cached against the current filter + variogram
  parameters so revisiting an earlier state is instant.
