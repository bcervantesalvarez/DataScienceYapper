A Python Random Forest model that classifies rare-disease patients into mild,
moderate, and severe categories from symptom flags. The non-obvious requirement:
the output had to be a handful of human-readable rules (e.g. `cramps + spasms ⇒
severe`) that physicians could sanity-check, not a black-box probability score.

Evaluated with confusion matrix, ROC AUC, precision-recall, and Cohen's kappa
to make sure the simplified rules didn't trade away too much performance.
