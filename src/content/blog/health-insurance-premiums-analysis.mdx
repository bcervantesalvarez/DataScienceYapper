A Python notebook quantifying how smoking and BMI drive up health-insurance
premiums by age and gender. The headline: male smokers pay roughly 408% more
than non-smokers, female smokers about 350% more, with additional surcharges
once BMI crosses 30.

Useful as a worked example of how to structure a small statistical investigation
end-to-end — exploratory plots, group-wise comparisons, regression, and the
actual sentence at the end that says what the data show.
