A walkthrough of using WebR to put R execution directly in the browser for
classroom labs. The argument is simple: when students can't install software,
or they're on a Chromebook, or a lab machine is locked down, the friction of
"open RStudio and run this" stops being negligible.

The post demonstrates a tidy-data wrangling lab built around WebR, where each
exercise re-runs live in the browser and students see results in milliseconds.
Cross-platform, zero-install, and the instructor doesn't spend the first
thirty minutes troubleshooting R installs.
