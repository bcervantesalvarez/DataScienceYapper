A teaching walkthrough of logistic regression and ANOVA on a 32,581-application
loan dataset. Income, home ownership, and loan amount come out as the headline
predictors of approval, with higher income raising the odds as expected.

The unexpected finding — credit score's coefficient runs the wrong direction in the
fitted model — turns into the talk's pedagogical anchor. We use it to discuss
specification error, omitted-variable bias, and why a coefficient sign that violates
intuition is a prompt to inspect the model, not to ship it.
