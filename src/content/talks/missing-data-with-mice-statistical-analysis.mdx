A practical session on handling missing data without pretending it doesn't exist.
Covers the three flavors of missingness — MCAR, MAR, and MNAR — and where each
naive approach (drop the row, mean-impute, fill forward) silently distorts your
downstream estimates.

The back half of the talk applies Multiple Imputation by Chained Equations via
the `mice` R package, with worked examples that show how to inspect convergence,
diagnose imputation quality, and propagate uncertainty through to the final
analysis.
