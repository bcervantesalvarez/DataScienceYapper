A graduate-level survival analysis exploring how water temperature modulates the
toxicity of a common pesticide on aquatic organisms. Cox proportional-hazards
models test whether higher temperatures meaningfully shorten survival times, with
particular attention to interaction effects between exposure level and ambient
conditions.

The applied angle: as freshwater systems warm, pesticide doses that were once
sub-lethal can start producing measurable mortality. The talk treats survival
modeling as a tool for quantifying that shift.
